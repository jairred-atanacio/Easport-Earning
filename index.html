Esports History

Evolution of Esports Prize Pools

The world of competitive gaming has witnessed unprecedented growth over the past two decades. From humble beginnings with prize pools of a few thousand dollars, esports has evolved into a billion-dollar industry with tournaments offering millions in prize money.

Major Milestones

2000 - The Beginning

The first major esports tournaments began with games like Quake and StarCraft, featuring prize pools ranging from $1,000 to $50,000.

2011 - The International Launch

Valve Corporation launched The International for Dota 2 with a groundbreaking $1.6 million prize pool, setting new standards for esports tournaments.

2019 - Fortnite World Cup

The Fortnite World Cup Solo Finals awarded $3 million to winner Bugha, the largest single-player prize in esports history.
